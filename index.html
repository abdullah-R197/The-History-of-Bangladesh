Historical Timeline

Before 1200 AD

Ancient Bengal

Bengal was part of ancient empires like Maurya Empire (321–185 BCE) and Gupta Empire (320–550 CE). Key cultural and trade center in South Asia.

Read More arrow_forward
1200–1757

Islamic Rule

Turkic and Afghan rulers established Islamic rule in Bengal. Bengal became a wealthy province under the Mughals with cultural fusion of traditions.

Read More arrow_forward
1757–1947

British Colonial Rule

Battle of Plassey (1757) marked the beginning of British rule. Bengal became the first British colony in India, facing exploitation and famine.

Read More arrow_forward
1947–1971

Partition and East Pakistan

1947 partition divided Bengal. East Pakistan faced economic exploitation and language discrimination, leading to the Language Movement of 1952.

Read More arrow_forward
1971

Liberation War

After 9 months of war following Operation Searchlight, Bangladesh achieved independence on December 16, 1971, with Indian intervention.

Read More arrow_forward
1972–Present

Modern Bangladesh

From post-independence challenges to becoming one of the fastest growing economies, Bangladesh has made significant progress in development.
